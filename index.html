About me

Barcelona Balboa Festival is an international festival organized by the BCNswing association, together with Gloria Garcia.
Here you will enjoy classes with personalized attention from international teachers with an innovative methodology that centres on the students.
You will have the best live swing music at parties and nonstop dancing. With good leader-follower balance to ensure maximum dancing opportunities for everyone.
There will also be time to enjoy the city and its attractions.

CLASSES & Teachers

PURE BAL TRACK

We’ll work on closed position Balboa techniques for all kinds of tempos including slow bal.

bal swing TRACK

We’ll work on open position balboa transitions to be able to diversify your basic bal-swing repertoire to the music.

We offer 4 hours of classes plus 1 hour lecture on Friday. Saturday and Sunday, students will get a 2 hours daily session having two extra teachers in one of them for more personalized attention. Group classes will be multilevel, will only work either on Pure Bal or Bal Swing and changing partner will be optional. Sunday’s class will be a continuation of Saturday’s material to make sure it is well integrated and works.

Corey & Susan Manke (USA)

Corey and Susan Manke are Balboa and Swing dancers from Nashville, Tennessee. While Lindy Hop was their first passion, they soon discovered their true love of Balboa in 2004 fascinated with its connection, rhythms and partnering. They have spent over a decade studying the dance and are particularly drawn to the aesthetic, connection and feel of the original dancers. They strive to implement these elements into their own dance style and reference these dancers in their teaching in order to preserve the original dancers’ contribution to the dance floor. They have placed in several US competitions but they enjoy the aspect of social dancing the most.

Cris Kay (SP) & Jon Kay (UK)

Since they started dancing together in 2007, Jon and Cris have travelled the world taking classes and teaching Balboa, Blues and Lindy Hop. While in Spain they have helped to form Swing & Co. in Zaragoza, Cris’s home town, and create an ever growing Swing dance scene where they are the ambassadors of Balboa. They are easily approachable both in class and on the social dance floor and they love to experiment with new teaching and dancing techniques and methodologies. As dancers they mix a cocktail of musicality, elegance and fun with a pinch of craziness. They have won several Balboa competitions in Europe and placed in the Great Lakes Balboa Escape Festival 2017 in Chicago and ABW 2019 in Cleveland.

Glòria Garcia (SP) &Mel Calanglang (UK)  

Mel is a dance enthusiast who has been dedicating his time and effort to encouraging Balboa in the United Kingdom. Being familiar with Lindy hop and Blues, his current passion is mastering Balboa and Bal-swing, a journey in continuous learning. Currently, you can find him social dancing and competing at various events across Europe and the United States together with Jo Calanglang, his dance partner with whom he also teaches. His “down-to-earth” and positive attitude helps to make the classes he teaches not only informative but also enjoyable. His aspirations to be ambassador of balboa stems from a continuous learning and sharing of his passion and knowledge of the dance to enthusiasts alike.
Coming from various dancing backgrounds like afro-latin and solo jazz, Gloria has been teaching internationally since 2010, winning various Balboa European and US titles and contributing in the development of this dance in Barcelona and Spain. She is also a Dance Movement Therapist (DMT) specialized in movement analysis, which she uses in her revolutionary Balboa teaching methodology that she has been developing since 2015. She is mainly interested in offering classes that significantly improve the quality of students movement as well as their leader and follower partnership and own style development as leader, follower and couple.

Enjoy with respect

BCNSwing believes Swing is live art form, and we wish to approach these artistic expressions with respect and appreciation. 
We believe that everyone has the right to enjoy these activities with complete peace of mind. and that everyone has a responsibility to build a safe environment and contribute to the well-being of the whole community.
 
No type of violent, intimidating, sexist, racist, or exclusionary behavior will be tolerated under any circumstances. If you see or experience any improper behavior during the festival please contact the organization team immediately or the purple spot called Punt lila, which you’ll find by the check in table, specially created for this.

The Organization commits itself to treat any report made with respect, sensitivity and confidentiality, whether from the dancers, from our local scene or from other places. Also, the organization reserves the right to expel anyone who acts inappropriately.

Dancing is a very physical activity and as such, everyone is responsible for their own physical safety and that of others around them. So, practice safe dance floor movements, apologize if you accidentally bump into another person, do not offer unsolicited advice or instructions, nor perform aerials on the social dance floor.

Please also take special hygiene care and review your personal odor because it is not pleasant to dance with someone who has bad body odor, bad breath or wears clothes with excessive sweat. We ask that you bring spare clothes and take extra care of your hygiene in order to avoid unpleasant moments for the other dancers.

By following this code of conduct, we will generate a much more pleasant environment for everyone where we can enjoy dancing and personal exchange.

Tips for tourists: stay safe and respect others

TRANSPORT

To move around Barcelona, you can buy a metro ticket T-Casual for 11.35€ which contains 10 single trips. And if you combine bus and metro within 45 min you will only spend one trip fare. 

If you are planning to travel to Barcelona by car, the easiest option is to ask at your hotel to book a parking space for you. There are also indoors and outdoors parking spaces where you can leave your car but they normally are a bit more expensive than the hotel ones. The cheapest option is to park in a free space close to Gracia area but they are hard to find. Also, we don’t advice you to use your car during the weekend if you are visiting the city or your accommodation is not in Gracia area.

From Barcelona airport you can take the metro, the train, the bus or a taxi and it will only take you between 20-35min to get to Barcelona center, except by metro, that will take you 1 hour. There is one train every 30 min and there are buses every 5 min but if you arrive at T1 you’ll need to take a free bus to T2 and then go to the train station. So, if your flight arrives at T1, we would advise you to take the bus to Barcelona center directly from there. From T1 you could also take the metro. Train will cost you 4.60€, metro 5.15€, bus (Aerobús) 6.75€ and taxi around 30€ and 40€. Once you are in the center, you can get to the workshop by metro going to Fontana station (green line).
